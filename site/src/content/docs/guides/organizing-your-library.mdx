Arcagrad reads your existing folders — you don't reorganize anything to get started, and
a flat pile of archives works fine. But a light folder convention unlocks nicer grouping,
and it's all just folders and files you already understand.

## One folder, one kind

Each top-level folder inside your library becomes a **kind** — a tab across the top of
your library. The folder name *is* the label, so you decide how it's grouped:

<FileTree>
- content/
  - Manga/
  - Comics/
  - Books/
</FileTree>

Anything sitting loose at the top level shows up under **Uncategorized**, so nothing is
ever hidden or rejected. Group by type, language, source — whatever suits you. The folders
are just labels.

<Aside type="tip" title="Bring your own structure">
Already have a flat library, or one organized by artist? Point Arcagrad at it as-is — it
all works, and anything outside a kind folder simply appears under *Uncategorized*. Add
kind folders later whenever you feel like tidying up.
</Aside>

## Series and standalone works

Inside a kind, the folder layout tells Arcagrad what's a series and what stands alone:

- A **subfolder is a series** — every archive inside it is a **volume**.
- A **loose archive is a standalone work** — a one-shot, an art book, a single issue.

<FileTree>
- Manga/
  - Berserk/
    - Berserk v01.cbz
    - Berserk v02.cbz
    - Berserk v03.cbz
  - A Standalone Story.cbz
</FileTree>

A series collapses to a single cover in your grid; open it to see its volumes in order.
Volume numbers come from the filenames (`v01`, `v02`, `Chapter 5`…), so consistent naming
keeps them sorted. Extra sub-folders for arcs or batches inside a series don't split it —
everything beneath the series folder belongs to the same series.

## The right reader, automatically

You never pick a "type" for a file. Arcagrad looks at each file's **contents**, not its
name, and chooses the reading experience: image archives (CBZ, CBR, and friends) page like
a comic, and EPUBs reflow like a book. A comic and a novel can sit in the same library, and
each opens the way it should.

## Adding and removing

Three ways to get items in:

- **Drop files into the folder** — Arcagrad notices and indexes them within seconds; covers
  fill in as it works.
- **Upload from the web app** — choose the kind, and it lands in the right folder for you.
- **Download with a plugin** — browse an external source and pull an item straight into your
  library.

Removing an item from the web app deletes the actual file. Arcagrad only ever adds or removes
*whole* files — it never rewrites the contents of one.

## Scanning

Arcagrad scans when it starts and then watches for changes, so new files appear on their own
— you don't run scans by hand. Folders that haven't changed are skipped, which is why even a
very large library re-checks quickly.
