Once your library is in, here's what using Arcagrad feels like day to day.

## Reading

Comics and manga open in a **page reader**; EPUB books open in a **reflowable reader** that
adapts to your screen. Either way, your place is saved automatically and **syncs across your
devices** — start on your laptop, pick up on your phone. A **Continue reading** shelf keeps
everything you're partway through in one place.

## Favorites and ratings

Mark anything as a **favorite**, and give items a **1–5 star rating**. Both are personal to
your account, so everyone sharing the server keeps their own.

## Search and tags

Search runs across **titles, creators, and tags**, with suggestions as you type. Tags are
**namespaced** — creators, characters, source works, content, and more — so you can tell a
character named "Nagi" from an artist named "Nagi", and filter by exactly what you mean.

<Aside type="tip">
Tags mostly come from [plugins](/guides/plugins/) — install one for a kind and your items pick
up a rich, searchable set of tags automatically.
</Aside>

## Recommendations

Every item has a **More like this** list, and your home page has a personal **For You** shelf
built from what you've read, favorited, and rated. Recommendations stay within a kind — so
manga suggests manga and books suggest books — and they lean on tags, so a well-tagged library
gives better picks.

## Following new releases

Subscribe to a search on an external source and Arcagrad collects new matches for you to review.
See [Plugins](/guides/plugins/) for how **Following** works.
